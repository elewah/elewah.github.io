Bio

Abdelrahman received his bachelor's degree in electrical engineering in 2012 and a master's degree in 2018 from Benha university. During taking his master's, he worked as a TA in the electrical department at Benha faculty of engineering. In 2019, Abdelrahman worked as a part-time TA in the computer science faculty in the Arab Academy for Science, Technology & Maritime Transport-Smart village. He has participated in innovation competitions that provide startup workshops to launch ideas in the market. In 2017, he had achieved the first winner in Mobile Application Launchpad (MAL) competition with his partners at the Technology and Innovation track. The proposed idea is to replace the lift buttons with a tablet running on Android OS. In other words, integrate the lift with android OS, then replace floors requesting by number keys with logos of companies on each floor or using a voice assistant to request the desired floor or the user destination. Recently, he, with his partners, has been selected to join a business-incubation program, InnoEgypt. They introduce Tamra, an IoT node that is low cost and has multi-connectivity, for the makers and developers. Currently, he is a Ph.D. student at Ontario tech university. He is interested in the 4th industrial revolution, so his research areas are the internet of things, machine learning, and cloud computing. He has participated in organizing the Egyptian Engineering Day (EED) for four years as a volunteer member of the public relations team.

Open-Source Repositories

Aug 2021

ThingsDriver is a new methodology in how we can govern IoT devices. When we connect a device such as a printer to the local personal computer (PC), the PC requires installing the printer driver to get the printer configurations. ThingsDriver adopts the same concept, where IoT devices in the thingsDriver architecture are extendable from the cloud, and the cloud needs the IoT devices driver to govern them. Looking at IoT devices as extendable components from the cloud can pave the way for a collaborative IoT environment.

Aug 2020

RadViz-Plotly is An Open-Source Python package that data scientists can use to draw the 2D, and 3D Radial Visualization (RadViz) plots for any high-dimensional datasets. Using the 3D/2D RadViz plot, we can have a broad vision for data distribution on all provided dimensions to understand the data and discover hidden insights.
