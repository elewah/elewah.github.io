Bio

For 6 years, he has worked as a teaching assistant in the Benha faculty of engineering at the electrical department. Learning and acquiring knowledge about revolutionary technologies are his interests, especially the technologies related to the 4th industrial revolution.

During his master's degree, he tried to implement a multi-modem system based on SDR (software-defined radio), using(USRP N210) (Universal Software Radio Peripheral) as SDR hardware and GNU radio as software Radio toolkit. The proposed system will be able to change the used modulation technique, depending on the channel state. He introduced Mth Root Mth Power SNR MPSK Estimator to estimate the received SNR, then request to change MPSK order, according to the SNR value to receive the signal with acceptable bit error rate in the cost of decreasing the transmission rate.

After taking his master's degree, he started to learn other worlds. He chose the IoT (Internet of Things); therefore, he gleaned, learned, and applied some of IoT applications. Besides, he has taken a cross-platform-mobile development course and attended business workshops about startup management.

Recently, he, with his partners, has been selected to join a business-incubation program, InnoEgypt. They introduce, Tamra, IoT node, that is low cost and high-connectivity efficiency, for the maker and developer.

Currently, he is studying for a Ph.D.'s degree at Ontario tech university.
