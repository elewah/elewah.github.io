Public Sensing As A Service

Data is the black gold of this era. Many applications use data as the keystone to what they provide. However, its dark side comes out due to the unethical usage of data.

At the individual scale: Many applications encourage users to spend more time browsing their content by offering suggestions based on their previous browsing history regardless of the user's usefulness. In addition to wasting their time, these users are also very destructive for their work and personal life. Moreover, studies show that people who spend too much time on their smartphones are more likely to suffer from depression. At the community level: A few companies dominate collecting data from users, businesses, firms, etc., almost everything. The current situation, for sure, is convenient for these companies. However, for small businesses, in the long run, It is a curse. For instance, in the E-commerce field, some companies get advantages of entering this field and have notable improvements in revenues. Nevertheless, for a while, they noticed that their selling operations were dropping since the E-commerce platform itself exploited companies' data and started offering their products at competitive prices. As a result, some of the small businesses lost the race and announced bankruptcy. 

The human race discovered black gold, petroleum, which looked stunning but subsequently observed climate change due to the excessive usage of petroleum substitutes. Replacing fossil fuels with renewable energies can mitigate the climate change crisis. In the same way, we can rebalance our ecosystem by implementing a Public Sensing-IoT-Network (PS-IoT-N) platform that protects companies' privacy and reduces the exploitation of individuals' data. For example, small business will have their own data warehouse. Whenever a user requests an order, this should be done through a private channel where each side keeps its data without involving a third party who could exploit it.

2- Public Sensing Components

  • ThingsDriver: Unified Interoperable Driver for IoT Nodes (UIDI)

ThingsDriver is a new methodology in how we can govern IoT devices. When we connect a device such as a printer to the local personal computer (PC), the PC requires installing the printer driver to get the printer configurations. ThingsDriver adopts the same concept, where IoT devices in the thingsDriver architecture are extendable from the cloud, and the cloud needs the IoT devices driver to govern them. Looking at IoT devices as extendable components from the cloud can pave the way for a collaborative IoT environment.
